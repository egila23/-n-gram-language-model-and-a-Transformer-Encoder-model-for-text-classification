   Answer: 135,645 unique 3-grams

5. How many possible 2- and 3-grams could there be, given the same vocabulary? How do the empirical counts compare to the number of possible grams? (2 points)
   
   Answer:
   For Business dataset (vocabulary size = 11,916):
   - Possible 2-grams: 11,916^2 = 141,991,056
   - Possible 3-grams: 11,916^3 = 1,691,965,423,296
   
   For Sports dataset (vocabulary size = 10,607):
   - Possible 2-grams: 10,607^2 = 112,508,449
   - Possible 3-grams: 10,607^3 = 1,193,377,118,543
   
   Comparison: The empirical counts are drastically smaller than the possible n-grams. 
   For business: 83,819 / 141,991,056 = 0.06% of possible 2-grams observed
                 141,221 / 1,691,965,423,296 = 0.000008% of possible 3-grams observed
   
   This demonstrates the sparsity problem in n-gram models - only a tiny fraction of 
   possible n-grams actually occur in natural language, which is why smoothing techniques 
   like Laplace smoothing are necessary.

4.4 Comparison to a GPT (14 points)
-----------------------------------

1. What is the perplexity of your N-gram model? (2 points)
   Answer: 177.48

2. What is the perplexity of the GPT-2 model? (2 points)
   Answer: 37.79

3. How might you reason about the differences in perplexity between these two models? (6 points)
   
   Answer: The GPT-2 model achieves significantly lower perplexity (37.79) compared to 
   the N-gram model (177.48). This difference can be explained by several factors:
   
   Parameters:
   - N-gram model: Has relatively few parameters (~10K-20K), limited to storing observed 
     n-gram counts and smoothed probabilities.
   - GPT-2 (DistilGPT-2): Has ~82 million parameters, allowing it to learn complex patterns 
     and representations.
   
   Vocabulary Size:
   - N-gram model: Limited to vocabulary seen in the training corpus, with smoothing for 
     unseen n-grams.
   - GPT-2: Uses BPE tokenization with ~50K subword tokens, enabling it to handle any input 
     text effectively, including rare and OOV words.
   
   Training Data:
   - N-gram model: Trained only on the small tech-small.txt file.
   - GPT-2: Pre-trained on WebText, a massive corpus of ~40GB of text from the internet, 
     giving it broad world knowledge and linguistic understanding.
   
   Context Window:
   - N-gram (trigram): Can only consider 2 previous tokens.
   - GPT-2: Can attend to up to 1024 tokens, capturing long-range dependencies.
   
   The combination of more parameters, larger training data, and longer context windows 
   allows GPT-2 to model language much more effectively, resulting in lower perplexity.

4. What are the trade-offs between a task-specific Transformer Encoder versus a large-scale generative model like GPT-2 for classification tasks? (4 points)
   
   Answer:
   
   Task-Specific Transformer Encoder:
   Advantages:
   - Smaller model size, faster inference, lower computational cost
   - Can be trained from scratch on domain-specific data
   - More interpretable attention patterns for analysis
   - Better suited for bidirectional understanding (looks at full context)
   - Lower memory requirements for deployment
   
   Disadvantages:
   - Requires labeled training data for the specific task
   - Limited generalization to new domains
   - May underperform when training data is scarce
   
   Large-Scale GPT-2:
   Advantages:
   - Pre-trained on massive data, captures broad linguistic knowledge
   - Can be fine-tuned with minimal task-specific data
   - Strong zero-shot and few-shot capabilities
   - Better handling of rare words and diverse vocabulary
   
   Disadvantages:
   - Much larger model, higher computational and memory costs
   - May be overkill for simple classification tasks
   - Autoregressive (left-to-right), not naturally suited for classification
   - Harder to interpret and explain predictions
   - Environmental cost of training and running large models
   
   For classification tasks specifically, a task-specific encoder is often preferred when 
   sufficient labeled data is available, as it provides a good balance of performance 
   and efficiency. GPT-2 style models are better when labeled data is limited or when 
   the task requires generating explanations alongside classifications.
